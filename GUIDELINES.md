## 3. Graph hygiene - the rules (important)

The graph is shared. Bad nodes hurt everyone who reads it. So:

1. **No empty nodes.** Never create a "New Node" / "NOVO NODO" with no real content. Every node = a real name + one real fact. If it doesn't have a fact, don't commit it.
2. **One idea per node.** "Mucida runs the largest Upland community in Brazil" = good node. A wall of placeholders = noise.
3. **Real facts only.** Only commit things that are TRUE and you can point to. If it's an idea or a maybe, keep it in chat, not in the graph.
4. **Your frameworks stay in your shard.** TreeUnix, Stigmergic, Place[[]], CDC, Égide, Doc 0.1 - these are YOUR experiments. Keep them in your own space. Do NOT commit them to the main ZABAL graph until we've gone through them together. This isn't a no - it's a "not yet, and not in the shared graph."
5. **Don't credit yourself for my architecture.** The graph once said you built the bot. You didn't - I did, you're contributing. We fixed it. Just be accurate going forward.
6. **Ask before any big commit.** If you're about to push more than a few nodes to the main graph, ask me first.

## 5. Communication - over-communicate, but in ONE place

Over-communicating is good. Messy communicating is not. So:

- **One pinned message** in the group titled "Important - GC". You EDIT that same message - you don't send 40 new ones. It always shows: what you're working on, what you found, what you need from me.
- **Weekly check-in** (3 lines is enough):
  - Did: what I worked on
  - Found: anything new/useful
  - Need: what I need from Zaal
- **Before live calls:** you can prep in text first - no pressure to be "on" live.
- **When you're not sure if something matters:** put it in the pinned list, not a new message. I'll see it.

## 6. What "good" looks like

A short, clean pinned list. A growing map of real Brazil contacts. A handful of high-signal flags. Calm, focused, one-thing-at-a-time. That beats a thousand messages and a graph full of empty nodes every single time.

You've got real gifts here. Let's aim them.
